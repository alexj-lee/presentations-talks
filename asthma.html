Asthma and dupilumab therapy

PSPG219

Asthma is one of the most common non-communicable diseases worldwide


High costs, high disparity

  • 2008-2013: $81.9 billion in costs (US) from asthma
  • for children, top cause of missed school days
  • some variability in estimates due to disease heterogeneity
  • disparities by sex, socioeconomic status, race/ethnicity

Historically, small molecules have been standard of care



Albuterol (salbutamol)

  • \(\beta_{2}\) adrenergic receptor agonist
  • causes dilation of bronchioles, vasodilation
  • long acting and short acting variants, some controversy over usage and efficacy


Inhaled corticosteroids

  • steroids help reduce inflammation
  • some overlap with drugs meant to treat eczema, dermatitis etc

Antibody therapy has potential to be more specific, effective

What is the target of dupilumab? the IL-4\(R\alpha\) (IL4-receptor)



  • receptor binds IL-4 and IL-13 \(\rightarrow\) activates iGE production
  • iGE \(\rightarrow\) mast cell histamine release \(\rightarrow\) B-cell, CD-4+ T-cells, eosinophils recruited
  • IL-4 and IL-13 also signals airway smooth muscle cells and epithelial cells

Dupilumab mechanism of action

How was dupilumab discovered?

VelocImmune platform for humanized antibody discovery


VelocImmune mice have human VDJ regions, but mouse constant and control regions



  • antibodies generated from mice are sometimes immunogenic

  • VelocImmune generates antibodies that are significantly less immunogenic, greatly reducing development time

  • Mice were injected with IL-4R\(\alpha\) receptor protein

Efficacy & safety [52 week, 1902 patient study]


FEV measures total lung volume exhaled in first second of exhalation test





Rate of severe asthma exacerbations was ~ half from placebo

Dupilumab Efficacy and Safety in Moderate-to-Severe Uncontrolled Asthma, Castro et al., NEJM 2018
